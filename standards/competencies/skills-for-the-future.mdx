Future-ready skills frameworks describe the capabilities students will need after they leave school. They overlap heavily with Portrait of a Graduate work, but they usually draw on labor-market research from groups like the World Economic Forum, OECD, and P21.

These frameworks help schools answer a real question that parents and boards keep asking: what is school actually preparing students to do? Most lists include some mix of communication, problem-solving, digital literacy, adaptability, and working with people.
