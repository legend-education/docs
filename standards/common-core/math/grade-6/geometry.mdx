# What are the Common Core Geometry standards for Grade 6?

<div class="answer-box">
  Geometry in grade 6 (`6.G`) extends area, surface area, and volume to new shapes and contexts. Students find the area of triangles, special quadrilaterals, and polygons by decomposing them (`6.G.A.1`), find the volume of a right rectangular prism with fractional edge lengths (`6.G.A.2`), draw polygons on the coordinate plane (`6.G.A.3`), and use nets to find surface area (`6.G.A.4`).
</div>

## What it covers

- Solve real-world and mathematical problems involving area, surface area, and volume (`6.G.A`).

## Classroom application

The decomposition work in `6.G.A.1` is the conceptual heart of this domain. The area of a triangle is half the area of the rectangle that contains it, by decomposing the rectangle into two congruent triangles. The area of a trapezoid is found by splitting it into a rectangle and one or two triangles, or by averaging the parallel sides. Students who can decompose shapes confidently arrive at high school geometry with most of the area work already done.

`6.G.A.2` extends the volume work from grade 5 to fractional edge lengths. A prism with edge lengths 2.5 by 3 by 4 has a volume of 30, which the same formula gives. The standard explicitly asks students to pack the prism with unit cubes of fractional side length to show that the formula still works.

`6.G.A.3` connects geometry to the coordinate plane work in `6.NS.C.8`. Polygons drawn from coordinates can have side lengths computed by subtracting the x-coordinates (for horizontal sides) or y-coordinates (for vertical sides). Diagonal sides come later, with the Pythagorean theorem in grade 8.

Nets (`6.G.A.4`) are flattened three-dimensional figures. The surface area of a rectangular prism is the sum of the areas of its six faces, which is easier to see when the prism is unfolded into a net. The net also previews the cone, cylinder, and pyramid surface-area work in middle school and high school.

`6.G` is a supporting domain, not the major work. It typically gets a focused unit of three to four weeks, often after the major work of `6.RP`, `6.NS`, and `6.EE`.
