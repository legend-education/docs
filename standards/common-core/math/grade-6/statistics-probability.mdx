# What are the Common Core Statistics & Probability standards for Grade 6?

<div class="answer-box">
  Statistics in grade 6 (`6.SP`) introduces the idea that a statistical question is one that anticipates variability in the data (`6.SP.A.1`). Students describe a distribution by shape, center, and spread (`6.SP.A.2`, `6.SP.A.3`), display data with dot plots, histograms, and box plots (`6.SP.B.4`), and summarize numerical data with measures of center (mean, median) and variability (range, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation) in context (`6.SP.B.5`).
</div>

## What it covers

- Develop understanding of statistical variability (`6.SP.A`).
- Summarize and describe distributions (`6.SP.B`).

## Classroom application

`6.SP.A.1` is the foundational standard of statistics. A statistical question expects variability. "How tall am I?" is not statistical (one answer). "How tall are the students in my class?" is statistical (many answers, with a distribution). The classroom move is a sorting routine where students sort cards as statistical or not, with reasons.

The shape, center, and spread framework (`6.SP.A.2`) is the lens for every distribution students see this year. Shape comes from a dot plot or histogram (skewed, symmetric, bimodal). Center is the mean or median. Spread is the range, interquartile range, or mean absolute deviation. Students should be able to describe a distribution in all three at once.

Mean as the "balance point" is a useful conceptual move. A dot plot of values with the mean marked balances on the fulcrum at the mean. This insight makes the mean's sensitivity to outliers visible (one extreme value tips the balance).

Box plots (`6.SP.B.4`) are new at this grade. The five-number summary (minimum, first quartile, median, third quartile, maximum) gives the box plot. Box plots are the highest-leverage display for comparing two distributions, which becomes a target in grade 7 (`7.SP.B.4`).

`6.SP` is a supporting domain. Most teachers teach it as a single unit of three to four weeks late in the year, after the major work of `6.RP`, `6.NS`, and `6.EE` is settled.
