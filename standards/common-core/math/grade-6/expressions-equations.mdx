# What are the Common Core Expressions & Equations standards for Grade 6?

<div class="answer-box">
  Expressions and Equations in grade 6 (`6.EE`) is the start of the algebra strand. Students write and evaluate expressions with exponents (`6.EE.A.1`) and variables (`6.EE.A.2`), generate equivalent expressions using the properties of operations (`6.EE.A.3`), and solve one-variable equations of the form `x + p = q` and `px = q` (`6.EE.B.7`). They write inequalities (`6.EE.B.8`) and represent quantitative relationships between dependent and independent variables (`6.EE.C.9`).

## What it covers

- Apply and extend previous understandings of arithmetic to algebraic expressions (`6.EE.A`).
- Reason about and solve one-variable equations and inequalities (`6.EE.B`).
- Represent and analyze quantitative relationships between dependent and independent variables (`6.EE.C`).

## Classroom application

`6.EE.A.2` is small but worth a careful lesson. The same letter can mean different things in different contexts. In 3x + 2, `x` is an unknown that can take any number. In the expression for the area of a rectangle, `x` might stand for a length. The grade 6 move is to name what each variable represents in the situation before writing the expression.

The distributive property (`6.EE.A.3`) is the same property students used in grade 3 with whole-number arithmetic. Now it acts on expressions with variables: 3(2 + x) = 6 + 3x. The conceptual connection is the same; the notation is new. An area model still works: a rectangle 3 by (2 + x) splits into a 3-by-2 and a 3-by-x.

For `6.EE.B.7`, the solution method should be "do the same thing to both sides," not a memorized rule. Equations of the form `x + p = q` are solved by subtracting `p` from both sides; equations of the form `px = q` are solved by dividing both sides by `p`. The standard limits to non-negative rational solutions, so the operations stay friendly.

`6.EE.C.9` introduces the dependent-and-independent-variable language that will run through every algebra and function course. The classroom routine is to take a real situation, name the two variables, write the equation, fill out a table, and plot the graph. The connection between these four representations is the foundation of `7.RP` and `8.F`.
