# What are the Common Core Operations & Algebraic Thinking standards for Kindergarten?

<div class="answer-box">
  The Operations and Algebraic Thinking domain (`K.OA`) is the kindergarten introduction to addition and subtraction. Students put two groups together and take groups apart within 10 (`K.OA.A.1`, `K.OA.A.2`), decompose numbers into pairs (`K.OA.A.3`), find the missing partner of any number to 10 (`K.OA.A.4`), and fluently add and subtract within 5 (`K.OA.A.5`). The work is concrete: objects, fingers, drawings, sounds, acting out, and only later, equations.
</div>

## What it covers

- Understand addition as putting together and adding to, and understand subtraction as taking apart and taking from (`K.OA.A`).

## Classroom application

A typical kindergarten daily problem uses a take-from or an add-to story within 10: "5 ducks were in the pond. 2 more came. How many ducks are in the pond now?" Students retell the story, act it out with counters or cubes, write a number sentence, and share strategies (`K.OA.A.2`).

The decomposition work in `K.OA.A.3` is the foundation for grade 1 addition strategies. A useful routine is "shake and spill." Five two-color counters get shaken in a cup and spilled. Students record the result as 5 = ___ + ___. Across many spills, the same total decomposes many ways. That insight feeds directly into making ten in grade 1 and into the part-part-whole reasoning that runs through all of arithmetic.

Pairs that make 10 (`K.OA.A.4`) deserve dedicated practice. The full set is small (1 + 9, 2 + 8, 3 + 7, 4 + 6, 5 + 5) and worth knowing fluently, because making ten is the engine of the grade 1 strategy work in `1.OA.C.6`.

Fluency within 5 (`K.OA.A.5`) is the kindergarten fluency target. By June, students should add and subtract within 5 quickly and reliably. This sets up the grade 1 fluency-within-10 target.
