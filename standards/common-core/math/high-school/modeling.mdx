# What are the Common Core high school Modeling standards?

<div class="answer-box">
  Modeling is the only high school conceptual category that has no domains, clusters, or new standards of its own. Instead, modeling standards are marked with a star symbol throughout the other categories (Number & Quantity, Algebra, Functions, Geometry, Statistics & Probability). Modeling is the practice of using mathematics to analyze real-world situations: making choices, building a model, interpreting results, validating, and revising.

## What modeling is

Modeling is the process of choosing and using appropriate mathematics to analyze a real situation. The Common Core describes a modeling cycle with six steps:

1. **Identify variables in the situation** and select those that represent essential features.
2. **Formulate a model** by creating and selecting geometric, graphical, tabular, algebraic, or statistical representations that describe relationships between the variables.
3. **Analyze and perform operations** on these relationships to draw conclusions.
4. **Interpret the results** in terms of the original situation.
5. **Validate the conclusions** by comparing them with the situation, then either improve the model or, if it is acceptable, report on the conclusions and the reasoning behind them.
6. **Report** on the conclusions and the reasoning behind them.

This is not a linear list. Real modeling loops back: results that do not match observations send the modeler back to formulation; new questions reopen the cycle.

## Classroom application

The modeling category is a teaching stance, not a content block. Teachers turn star standards into modeling lessons by structuring the work around the six-step cycle.

A canonical example is a unit on car-loan or college-loan affordability. Students start with a description: "Suppose you take out a $20,000 loan at 5 percent annual interest for 5 years, with monthly payments." Step 1 identifies variables: principal, rate, term, monthly payment, total interest. Step 2 formulates: the loan-payment formula, drawn from the geometric series sum (`A-SSE.B.4` for advanced tracks, but the formula is teachable without proof). Step 3 analyzes: compute monthly payment, total paid, and total interest. Step 4 interprets: a $377 monthly payment, $22,645 total paid, $2,645 in interest. Step 5 validates: does this match online calculators? What happens if the rate changes? What if the term doubles? Step 6 reports.

Other rich modeling tasks include: predicting epidemic growth with exponential and logistic functions, designing the cheapest packaging for a fixed volume (geometry plus optimization), fitting a sine function to tides, and evaluating a published claim about test scores. Each can be staged over multiple class periods.

The key contrast with traditional word problems is that modeling problems are open. There is more than one valid model. Students must make assumptions and explain them. The validation step often reveals that the first model was too simple; iteration is expected, not a failure.

## Common teacher questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Modeling a separate course or unit?">
    No. Modeling is a category that overlays the other four (Number & Quantity, Algebra, Functions, Geometry, Statistics & Probability). Star-marked standards appear in those categories and are taught in context. Some schools offer a dedicated modeling course as a capstone, but it is not required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How is modeling different from word problems?">
    Word problems usually have a single intended path: read the problem, identify the formula, plug in, get the answer. Modeling problems are open: students decide what to model, what to leave out, and what assumptions to make. Validation is part of the work. Two students may produce two different valid models with different numerical answers, both correct given their assumptions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does modeling connect to MP4?">
    MP4 (Model with mathematics) is the Standards for Mathematical Practice version of this category. The high-school Modeling category gives MP4 specific content: which standards to use as raw material for modeling, and the six-step cycle as the process structure. Together, MP4 and Modeling describe the same teaching arc from different angles.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How is modeling assessed?">
    Modeling is hardest to assess with timed multiple-choice items. Many state assessments include short modeling tasks (one or two class periods) or extended performance tasks (multi-day). Rubric language emphasizes the choices students make and the justifications they provide, not just the numerical answer.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
