  Common Core Math runs from kindergarten through grade 12 with two parts that work together. The 8 Standards for Mathematical Practice (`MP1` to `MP8`) name the habits of mind students build at every grade. The content standards say what students should know and be able to do, organized by grade for K-8 (with domains like Operations and Algebraic Thinking, Number and Operations in Base Ten, and Geometry) and by 6 conceptual categories for high school (Number and Quantity, Algebra, Functions, Modeling, Geometry, Statistics and Probability).

## The two parts of CCSS Math

| Part | What it is | How it shows up |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Standards for Mathematical Practice** | 8 habits of mind that apply at every grade, `MP1` to `MP8`. | The same 8 practices, K through grade 12. |
| **Content standards** | What students should know and be able to do. | K-8 by grade, organized into domains. High school by 6 conceptual categories. |

The practices and the content live side by side. A grade 4 student practicing `MP3` (construct viable arguments) is doing it on the grade 4 content (multi-digit multiplication, fraction equivalence). A grade 11 student practicing the same `MP3` is doing it on quadratic functions. The habit is the same. The math grows up.

## How the standards are organized

Each grade in K-8 is broken into **domains**. A domain is a big topic area, like Geometry or Number and Operations in Base Ten. The exact set of domains shifts as students get older: kindergarten has Counting and Cardinality, grade 6 introduces Ratios and Proportional Relationships, grade 8 introduces Functions. Inside each domain, standards are grouped into **clusters** with a heading sentence (for example, "Represent and solve problems involving addition and subtraction"). The standards under that heading are the specific skills.

High school is different. There is no grade 9, grade 10, grade 11, or grade 12 math in the standards document. Instead, all high school math sits in 6 conceptual categories. Schools build their own course sequences (Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II, or an integrated pathway) on top of the categories.

A small number of high school standards are marked with a `(+)`. Those are advanced standards intended for students taking precalculus or other advanced courses, not required for college and career readiness in math.

## Major work of the grade

Common Core Math also flags **major work** at each grade. About 65 to 85 percent of instructional time should sit on the major work of the grade, with supporting and additional work filling the rest. The major work names what state tests focus on and what the next grade assumes students arrive knowing.

| Grade | Major work focus |
| --- | --- |
| K | Counting, comparing, and the operations of addition and subtraction within 10. |
| 1 | Addition and subtraction within 20, place value to 100, linear measurement. |
| 2 | Place value to 1000, addition and subtraction within 100, length measurement. |
| 3 | Multiplication and division within 100, fractions as numbers. |
| 4 | Multi-digit multiplication, fraction equivalence and operations, place value. |
| 5 | Operations with fractions and decimals, volume. |
| 6 | Ratios and rates, division of fractions, expressions and equations. |
| 7 | Proportional relationships, rational number arithmetic, expressions and equations. |
| 8 | Linear equations, functions, the Pythagorean theorem. |

## How to read a math code

The prefix tells you the grade, the domain, the cluster, and the standard. Once you can read the prefix, you can find any standard fast.

| Prefix | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `K`, `1`, `2`...`8` | Grade level. |
| `HSN`, `HSA`, `HSF`, `HSG`, `HSS` | High school conceptual category (Number and Quantity, Algebra, Functions, Geometry, Statistics and Probability). |
| `CC` | Counting and Cardinality (kindergarten only). |
| `OA` | Operations and Algebraic Thinking (K to grade 5). |
| `NBT` | Number and Operations in Base Ten (K to grade 5). |
| `NF` | Number and Operations: Fractions (grades 3 to 5). |
| `MD` | Measurement and Data (K to grade 5). |
| `G` | Geometry (K to high school). |
| `RP` | Ratios and Proportional Relationships (grades 6 and 7). |
| `NS` | The Number System (grades 6 to 8). |
| `EE` | Expressions and Equations (grades 6 to 8). |
| `F` | Functions (grade 8 and high school). |
| `SP` | Statistics and Probability (grades 6 to 8 and high school). |
| `MP` | Standards for Mathematical Practice (K to grade 12). |

### Worked example

```text
CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.3.OA.A.1
└────┬────┘ └──┬──┘ │ │ │
     │         │    │ │ └── standard number within the cluster
     │         │    │ └──── cluster letter
     │         │    └────── domain (OA = Operations and Algebraic Thinking)
     │         └─────────── grade level (3)
     └───────────────────── framework
```

Read this as the first standard in cluster A of the Operations and Algebraic Thinking domain for grade 3. The standard asks students to interpret products of whole numbers, for example, interpret 5 x 7 as the total number of objects in 5 groups of 7 objects each.

<Tip>
  You will often see math codes written without the `CCSS.MATH.CONTENT` prefix. `3.OA.A.1` and `CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.3.OA.A.1` point to the same standard.
</Tip>
