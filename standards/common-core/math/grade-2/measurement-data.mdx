# What are the Common Core Measurement & Data standards for Grade 2?

<div class="answer-box">
  Measurement and Data in grade 2 (`2.MD`) moves length to standard units (inches, feet, centimeters, meters), introduces the number line as a representation for whole-number sums and differences (`2.MD.B.6`), brings time to the nearest five minutes (`2.MD.C.7`), introduces money problems with dollar and cent symbols (`2.MD.C.8`), and extends data to picture graphs and bar graphs with up to four categories (`2.MD.D.10`).

## What it covers

- Measure and estimate lengths in standard units (`2.MD.A`).
- Relate addition and subtraction to length (`2.MD.B`).
- Work with time and money (`2.MD.C`).
- Represent and interpret data (`2.MD.D`).

## Classroom application

The "measure twice with different units" piece of `2.MD.A.2` is one of the most quietly important grade 2 standards. A pencil that is 6 inches long is also about 15 centimeters long. The same length, two units. Students notice that smaller units give more units. That insight is the seed of every unit conversion problem from here on.

The number line work in `2.MD.B.6` is the bridge from "length" to "number line as a representation of number." A 0-to-100 number line lets students show 47 + 28 as a jump from 47 by 20 to 67 and then by 8 to 75. The same line shows subtraction as the distance between two points. Both moves transfer to grade 3 work in `3.NF.A.2` (fractions on the number line).

For `2.MD.C.7`, count by fives around the clock face. Pair "the 1" with 5, "the 2" with 10, "the 3" with 15, and so on. Once that pairing is automatic, telling time to the nearest 5 minutes becomes a place value-style routine.

For `2.MD.C.8`, money is a context for `2.OA.A.1` and `2.NBT.B.5`. A word problem with quarters and dimes is an addition problem dressed in coins. Use that to your advantage: the same tape diagrams that work for `2.OA.A.1` work for money problems.
