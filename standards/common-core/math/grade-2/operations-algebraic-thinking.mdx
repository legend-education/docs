# What are the Common Core Operations & Algebraic Thinking standards for Grade 2?

<div class="answer-box">
  Operations and Algebraic Thinking in grade 2 (`2.OA`) takes addition and subtraction to one-step and two-step word problems within 100 (`2.OA.A.1`), brings fact fluency within 20 to mastery (`2.OA.B.2`), and introduces equal groups through even-and-odd reasoning (`2.OA.C.3`) and small rectangular arrays (`2.OA.C.4`). The array work is the bridge to grade 3 multiplication.

## What it covers

- Represent and solve problems involving addition and subtraction (`2.OA.A`).
- Add and subtract within 20 (`2.OA.B`).
- Work with equal groups of objects to gain foundations for multiplication (`2.OA.C`).

## Classroom application

Two-step problems (`2.OA.A.1`) are the harder addition this year. A useful routine: label the question, identify what you will find first, find that, then use it to find what the question asks. Tape diagrams help here. A student draws the whole, marks what is known, marks the missing piece in step 1, and then uses step 1's result to solve step 2.

Fluency within 20 (`2.OA.B.2`) is a year-long target. "Know from memory all sums of two one-digit numbers" is the harder part. Most students reach it through repeated meaningful use: number talks, fact fluency routines that include strategies (doubles, near doubles, making ten), and assessments that respect the year-long pace.

Even and odd (`2.OA.C.3`) is more important than it looks. The standard asks students to write an even number as a sum of two equal addends (8 = 4 + 4). That equation is the start of multiplication thinking. A class that can write 8 = 4 + 4 and 10 = 5 + 5 is one step away from 4 + 4 = 2 x 4 in grade 3.

Arrays (`2.OA.C.4`) should be experienced physically. Students lay out 3 rows of 4 counters, count the total, and write 4 + 4 + 4 = 12. Several days of this work, with the rows or columns getting added in different orders, builds the structure that grade 3 multiplication runs on.
