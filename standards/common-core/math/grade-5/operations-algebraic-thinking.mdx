# What are the Common Core Operations & Algebraic Thinking standards for Grade 5?

<div class="answer-box">
  Operations and Algebraic Thinking in grade 5 (`5.OA`) is small. Students use parentheses, brackets, and braces in numerical expressions and evaluate them (`5.OA.A.1`), write expressions that record calculations and interpret them without evaluating (`5.OA.A.2`), and analyze two numerical patterns built from two rules by forming ordered pairs and graphing them on the coordinate plane (`5.OA.B.3`). This domain is the bridge from elementary arithmetic to grade 6 expressions and equations.

## What it covers

- Write and interpret numerical expressions (`5.OA.A`).
- Analyze patterns and relationships (`5.OA.B`).

## Classroom application

The grouping symbols work in `5.OA.A.1` is best taught against the order of operations. Students see that the parentheses force a calculation first, and that brackets and braces nest around parentheses. A useful routine is to give the same numbers with different grouping symbols and ask students to predict (and explain) what the value will be.

`5.OA.A.2` is the standard that pushes against the urge to compute. The point is reading the structure of an expression and using it. 3 x (18932 + 921) is three times the sum, without computing the sum. That move is `MP7` (look for and make use of structure) and previews the grade 6 expressions work.

`5.OA.B.3` connects patterns to the coordinate plane (the new tool of the year). Two rules generate two sequences; the pairs of corresponding terms graph as points on a line. The relationship between the rules ("Add 6 gives terms that are twice the Add 3 terms") shows up as the slope between consecutive points. The standard does not name slope, but the work is the foundation of `6.RP` and `7.RP` proportional reasoning.

`5.OA` is a supporting domain, not the major work of grade 5. Most teachers treat it as a two-to-three-week unit that bridges decimal arithmetic and fraction operations, or weave the three standards through the year.
