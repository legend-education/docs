# What are the Standards for Mathematical Practice?

<div class="answer-box">
  The 8 Standards for Mathematical Practice (`MP1` to `MP8`) describe how students do math, not what math they do. They name the habits of mind a student should develop K through 12: making sense of problems, reasoning, modeling, using tools, attending to precision, and looking for structure. The same 8 practices apply at every grade, paired with the grade-level content. Strong math teaching uses the practices as the verbs and the content standards as the nouns.

## What it covers

- `MP1`: Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them.
- `MP2`: Reason abstractly and quantitatively.
- `MP3`: Construct viable arguments and critique the reasoning of others.
- `MP4`: Model with mathematics.
- `MP5`: Use appropriate tools strategically.
- `MP6`: Attend to precision.
- `MP7`: Look for and make use of structure.
- `MP8`: Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning.

## How the 8 practices fit together

The authors of the practices grouped them in pairs that share a habit:

| Pair | What the pair is about |
| --- | --- |
| `MP1` + `MP6` | Overarching habits: making sense of the problem, and being careful with definitions, units, and notation. |
| `MP2` + `MP3` | Reasoning and explaining: moving between concrete situations and symbolic representations, and arguing for or against a claim. |
| `MP4` + `MP5` | Modeling and using tools: representing a real situation mathematically and picking the right tool for the job. |
| `MP7` + `MP8` | Seeing structure and generalizing: noticing patterns inside a problem and noticing patterns across many problems. |

## Classroom application

Use the practices as the verbs of your math lessons and the content standards as the nouns. A daily lesson plan that says "Today students will practice multi-digit multiplication" describes a content target. A plan that says "Today students will construct an argument (`MP3`) for which multi-digit multiplication strategy is most efficient" connects content with a practice.

Most teachers find it cleaner to focus on one or two practices per unit rather than chasing all 8 every day. The pair groupings above are a useful starting point. A measurement unit naturally hits `MP6` (attend to precision). A modeling unit on rates leans on `MP4`. A unit on patterns and tables leans on `MP7` and `MP8`.

The practices also give a vocabulary for student feedback. Saying, "Your explanation does not yet meet `MP3`. You stated what you did, but not why it works," is more specific than "Add more detail."
