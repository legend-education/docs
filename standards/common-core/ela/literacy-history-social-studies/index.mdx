# What are the Common Core Literacy in History/Social Studies standards?

<div class="answer-box">
The Literacy in History/Social Studies standards live inside the Common Core ELA document but are written for content teachers. Reading (`RH.6-12`) covers working with primary and secondary sources: citing evidence, finding the central idea, judging an author's purpose. Writing (`WHST.6-12`) covers argument and informative writing inside history class, plus short and sustained research. The standards run on three bands: grades 6-8, 9-10, and 11-12. Elementary social studies sits outside this set.
</div>

## How the standards are organized

There are two strands. Reading in History/Social Studies (`RH`) describes how students read primary and secondary sources inside a history classroom. Writing in History, Science, and Technical Subjects (`WHST`) describes how they write inside those content classrooms.

`RH` is history-specific. `WHST` is shared with science and technical subjects, so a single set of writing standards covers all three. There is no narrative writing standard in `WHST`. Narrative writing belongs to the ELA standards.

Both strands start in grade 6 and run through grade 12, organized into three bands.

| Band | Reading code | Writing code | Where it shows up |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Grades 6-8 | `RH.6-8` | `WHST.6-8` | Middle school history and social studies. |
| Grades 9-10 | `RH.9-10` | `WHST.9-10` | World history, U.S. history I, civics, geography. |
| Grades 11-12 | `RH.11-12` | `WHST.11-12` | U.S. history II, government, economics, AP history courses. |

## How to read an RH or WHST code

The code names the strand and the band. Once you can read the prefix, you can find any standard fast.

```text
RH.9-10.1
тФФтФмтФШ тФФтФАтФмтФАтФШ тФВ
 тФВ    тФВ   тФФтФАтФА standard number within the strand
 тФВ    тФФтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФА grade band (9-10)
 тФФтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФА strand (RH = Reading in History/Social Studies)
```

Read this as the first reading standard for grades 9-10 history. The standard asks students to cite specific textual evidence and attend to the date and origin of the source.

```text
WHST.6-8.1
тФФтФАтФмтФАтФШ тФФтФмтФШ тФВ
  тФВ    тФВ  тФФтФАтФА standard number within the strand
  тФВ    тФФтФАтФАтФАтФА grade band (6-8)
  тФФтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФАтФА strand (WHST = Writing in History, Science, and Technical Subjects)
```

Read this as the first writing standard for middle school history, science, and technical subjects. The standard asks students to write a discipline-specific argument with claim, reasons, evidence, and a conclusion.

<Tip>
You will often see codes without the `CCSS.ELA-LITERACY` prefix on teacher-built materials. `RH.9-10.1` and `CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RH.9-10.1` point to the same standard.
</Tip>

## How RH and WHST ladder up to the CCRA anchors

Every `RH` and `WHST` standard maps to a College and Career Readiness anchor. The anchor names the K-12 endpoint; the band standard says what that endpoint looks like in a content classroom.

| Code | Anchor | What the anchor names |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `RH.x.1` | `CCRA.R.1` | Cite textual evidence. |
| `RH.x.2` | `CCRA.R.2` | Central ideas and themes. |
| `RH.x.3` | `CCRA.R.3` | How events and ideas develop. |
| `RH.x.4` | `CCRA.R.4` | Word choice in context. |
| `RH.x.5` | `CCRA.R.5` | Text structure. |
| `RH.x.6` | `CCRA.R.6` | Point of view and purpose. |
| `RH.x.7` | `CCRA.R.7` | Integrate information across media. |
| `RH.x.8` | `CCRA.R.8` | Evaluate arguments and claims. |
| `RH.x.9` | `CCRA.R.9` | Compare two or more sources on a shared topic. |
| `RH.x.10` | `CCRA.R.10` | Read complex text independently. |
| `WHST.x.1` | `CCRA.W.1` | Argument writing. |
| `WHST.x.2` | `CCRA.W.2` | Informative or explanatory writing. |
| `WHST.x.4` | `CCRA.W.4` | Match writing to task and audience. |
| `WHST.x.5` | `CCRA.W.5` | Plan and revise. |
| `WHST.x.6` | `CCRA.W.6` | Use digital tools to publish. |
| `WHST.x.7` | `CCRA.W.7` | Short and sustained research. |
| `WHST.x.8` | `CCRA.W.8` | Source evaluation and citation. |
| `WHST.x.9` | `CCRA.W.9` | Pull evidence from sources. |
| `WHST.x.10` | `CCRA.W.10` | Write routinely over short and long time frames. |

<Note>
There is no `WHST.x.3`. Narrative writing (`CCRA.W.3`) belongs to the ELA strand only. In history and science, students write argument and informative pieces.
</Note>

## Common teacher questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who owns these standards, the ELA teacher or the content teacher?">
    The content teacher. `RH` and `WHST` describe reading and writing inside a history classroom, not inside ELA. The standards live in the ELA document because the writers wanted reading and writing to keep growing after grade 5, when ELA stops being a separate subject for many students. Most schools share the work. The ELA team teaches argument structure and citation conventions. The social studies team teaches how to read a primary source and write a source-based argument on a history prompt.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do these standards replace my state's social studies standards?">
    No. State social studies standards say what content students should learn: U.S. history through Reconstruction, world geography, civics. `RH` and `WHST` say how students should read and write inside that content. The two work together. State content standards set the topic; the literacy standards set the reading and writing bar.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What about social studies in K-5?">
    Common Core does not have content-area literacy standards for K-5. Below grade 6, reading and writing in social studies fall under the main ELA standards (`RI` for informational reading and `W` for writing). Elementary students still read about communities, geography, and U.S. history, but the reading and writing expectations come from the K-5 ELA standards, not from `RH` or `WHST`. The C3 Framework picks up the content side at the elementary level.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
