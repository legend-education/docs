## The four strands at a glance

| Strand | Code prefix | Anchor count | What it covers |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Reading** | `R` (with `RL`, `RI`, `RF`) | 10 anchors (`CCRA.R.1-10`) | Comprehending literature and informational text. K-5 also covers foundational reading skills like phonics and fluency. |
| **Writing** | `W` | 10 anchors (`CCRA.W.1-10`) | Three writing types: argument, informative or explanatory, and narrative. Includes research and revision. |
| **Speaking and Listening** | `SL` | 6 anchors (`CCRA.S.1-6`) | Comprehension and collaboration, plus presentation of ideas and information. |
| **Language** | `L` | 6 anchors (`CCRA.L.1-6`) | Grammar and conventions, plus vocabulary acquisition and word use. |

## How the standards are organized

Every Common Core ELA standard ladders up to one of 32 College and Career Readiness anchor standards. The anchor names the K-12 endpoint. The grade-level standard underneath says what that endpoint looks like at kindergarten, grade 1, grade 2, and so on. Read together, they trace a single skill from age five to age eighteen.

K-5 has an extra strand called Reading Foundational Skills (`RF`). It covers print concepts, phonological awareness, phonics, word recognition, and fluency. `RF` does not exist past grade 5. By grade 6, students are expected to apply those skills inside the main Reading strand.

Grades 6-12 add literacy standards for history, social studies, science, and technical subjects. These standards live inside the ELA document but are written for content teachers. A middle school science teacher and a middle school ELA teacher share responsibility for them.

High school is written in two-year bands, 9-10 and 11-12. Each band describes what students should be able to do by the end of grade 10 or grade 12. Schools build their own course sequences (English I, English II, English III, English IV) on top of the bands.

## How to read an ELA code

The prefix tells you which strand and which grade. Once you can read the prefix, you can find any standard fast.

| Prefix | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `CCRA.R` | Anchor standards for Reading (10 total). |
| `CCRA.W` | Anchor standards for Writing (10 total). |
| `CCRA.S` | Anchor standards for Speaking and Listening (6 total). |
| `CCRA.L` | Anchor standards for Language (6 total). |
| `RL` | Grade-level Reading: Literature. |
| `RI` | Grade-level Reading: Informational Text. |
| `RF` | Reading: Foundational Skills (K-5 only). |
| `W` | Grade-level Writing. |
| `SL` | Grade-level Speaking and Listening. |
| `L` | Grade-level Language. |
| `RH` | Reading in History/Social Studies (grades 6-12). |
| `RST` | Reading in Science and Technical Subjects (grades 6-12). |
| `WHST` | Writing in History/Social Studies, Science, and Technical Subjects (grades 6-12). |

### Worked example

```text
CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RI.4.2
└──┬──┘ └────┬───┘ │ │ │
   │         │     │ │ └── standard number within the strand
   │         │     │ └──── grade level (4)
   │         │     └────── strand (RI = Reading: Informational Text)
   │         └──────────── content area (literacy)
   └────────────────────── framework
```

Read this as the second informational-reading standard for grade 4. The standard asks students to determine the main idea of a text and explain how it is supported by key details.

<Tip>
  You will often see ELA codes without the `CCSS.ELA-LITERACY` prefix on teacher-built materials. `RI.4.2` and `CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RI.4.2` point to the same standard.
</Tip>
