# What are the Common Core Writing anchor standards?

<div class="answer-box">
  The 10 Writing anchor standards (CCRA.W\.1-10) describe what students should be able to do as writers by the end of grade 12. The first three name the writing types students produce every year: argument, informative or explanatory, and narrative. The rest cover revision, research, source use, and writing on both tight deadlines and longer projects. Every grade-level writing standard, K through 12, ladders up to one anchor.
</div>

## What it covers

- CCRA.W.1: Write arguments with valid reasoning and evidence.
- CCRA.W.2: Write informative or explanatory texts.
- CCRA.W.3: Write narratives.
- CCRA.W.4: Match writing to its task and audience.
- CCRA.W.5: Revise and edit drafts.
- CCRA.W.6: Use digital tools to publish and collaborate.
- CCRA.W.7: Conduct short and sustained research projects.
- CCRA.W.8: Gather and integrate sources without plagiarism.
- CCRA.W.9: Draw evidence from texts to support writing.
- CCRA.W.10: Write routinely over short and long time frames.

## Classroom application

Use the anchors to design assignments and rubrics that line up across grades. The three writing types appear every year, with the bar rising at each grade. `CCRA.W.1-3` name the types. `CCRA.W.4-6` cover production and publishing. `CCRA.W.7-9` cover research and use of sources. `CCRA.W.10` covers writing volume across the year.

When each rubric criterion ties back to one anchor, feedback stays consistent across the team and is easier to defend in parent conversations.
