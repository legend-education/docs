# What are the Common Core Writing anchor standards?

<div class="answer-box">
The ten Writing anchor standards (CCRA.W.1-10) name what every K-12 student should be able to do as a writer. They cover three writing types (argument, informative or explanatory, and narrative), plus production and revision, digital publishing, research, source use, and writing routinely over short and long time frames. Every grade-level Writing standard, kindergarten through grade 12, ladders up to one of these ten anchors.

## What it covers

- CCRA.W.1: Write arguments with valid reasoning and evidence.
- CCRA.W.2: Write informative or explanatory texts.
- CCRA.W.3: Write narratives.
- CCRA.W.4: Match writing to its task and audience.
- CCRA.W.5: Revise and edit drafts.
- CCRA.W.6: Use digital tools to publish and collaborate.
- CCRA.W.7: Conduct short and sustained research projects.
- CCRA.W.8: Gather and integrate sources without plagiarism.
- CCRA.W.9: Draw evidence from texts to support writing.
- CCRA.W.10: Write routinely over short and long time frames.

## Classroom application

Teachers use the Writing anchors to design assignments and rubrics that line up across grades. The three writing types (argument, informative or explanatory, and narrative) show up every year, with the bar rising at each grade. CCRA.W.1-3 name those types. CCRA.W.4-6 cover production and digital publishing, and CCRA.W.7-9 cover research and use of sources. CCRA.W.10 covers writing volume across the year. When you build a writing rubric, each criterion can tie back to one anchor, which keeps feedback consistent across the team and easy to defend in parent conversations.
