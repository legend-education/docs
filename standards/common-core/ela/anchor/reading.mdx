# What are the Common Core Reading anchor standards?

<div class="answer-box">
The 10 Reading anchor standards (CCRA.R.1-10) describe what students should be able to do with any text by the end of grade 12. The skills move from citing evidence and finding central ideas to analyzing structure, weighing an author's argument, and reading complex texts independently. Every grade-level reading standard, K through 12, whether it's Literature (RL) or Informational Text (RI), ladders up to one of these 10 anchors.

## What it covers

- CCRA.R.1: Cite textual evidence.
- CCRA.R.2: Identify central ideas and themes.
- CCRA.R.3: Analyze how characters, events, and ideas develop.
- CCRA.R.4: Interpret word choice in context.
- CCRA.R.5: Analyze text structure.
- CCRA.R.6: Assess point of view and purpose.
- CCRA.R.7: Integrate information across media.
- CCRA.R.8: Evaluate arguments and claims.
- CCRA.R.9: Compare two or more texts on a shared theme.
- CCRA.R.10: Read complex literary and informational text independently.

## Classroom application

Use the anchors as the spine of your reading rubric. The anchor names the K-12 target. The grade-level RL and RI standards underneath say what that target looks like in your grade. When each rubric criterion maps back to one anchor, vertical alignment with the grade below and above is easy to defend.

Most state assessments report reading scores by anchor (citing evidence, central idea, structure, and so on). Anchor-aligned feedback lines up with the data families and principals already see.
