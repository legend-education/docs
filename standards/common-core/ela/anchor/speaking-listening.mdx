# What are the Common Core Speaking & Listening anchor standards?

<div class="answer-box">
  The 6 Speaking and Listening anchor standards (CCRA.S.1-6) describe what students should be able to do in discussions and presentations by the end of grade 12. The first three cover the listening side: joining a conversation and weighing what a speaker is actually claiming. The last three cover the speaking side: delivering findings clearly and adjusting speech for the audience. Every grade-level SL standard, K through 12, ladders up to one anchor.
</div>

## What it covers

- CCRA.S.1: Participate in discussions and build on others' ideas.
- CCRA.S.2: Evaluate information across media and formats.
- CCRA.S.3: Evaluate a speaker's reasoning and evidence.
- CCRA.S.4: Present findings clearly to an audience.
- CCRA.S.5: Use digital media and visuals to support presentations.
- CCRA.S.6: Adapt speech to context, including formal English.

## Standards

## Classroom application

Plan discussions and presentations alongside reading and writing. `CCRA.S.1-3` cover the listening side: comprehending information and evaluating speakers. `CCRA.S.4-6` cover the speaking side: presenting findings and adapting speech to the audience.

State assessments rarely test this strand directly. It shows up most in classroom rubrics for Socratic seminars and oral defenses rather than on state tests.
