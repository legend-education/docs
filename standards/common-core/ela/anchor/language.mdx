# What are the Common Core Language anchor standards?

<div class="answer-box">
  The 6 Language anchor standards (CCRA.L.1-6) describe what students should know about the grammar and vocabulary of English by the end of grade 12. The first three cover grammar, mechanics, and language choice. The last three cover vocabulary: working out unfamiliar words, understanding figurative language, and building academic vocabulary. Every grade-level L standard, K through 12, ladders up to one anchor.

## What it covers

- CCRA.L.1: Use standard English grammar and usage.
- CCRA.L.2: Use standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling.
- CCRA.L.3: Make language choices that fit the context.
- CCRA.L.4: Figure out the meaning of unfamiliar words.
- CCRA.L.5: Understand figurative language and word relationships.
- CCRA.L.6: Build and use academic and domain-specific vocabulary.

## Standards

## Classroom application

Plan vocabulary instruction and writing conventions together. `CCRA.L.1-2` cover grammar and conventions and usually live in writing conferences and editing checklists. `CCRA.L.3` covers stylistic choice and shows up in revision rubrics. `CCRA.L.4-6` cover vocabulary: figuring out new words, understanding figurative language, and using academic vocabulary across subjects.

Most state ELA tests embed `L.4-6` inside reading items, so vocabulary instruction lands hardest when it sits inside reading and writing lessons rather than as a separate block.
