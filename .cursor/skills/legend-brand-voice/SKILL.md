# Skill: Legend Brand Writing for Teachers

## Purpose

Write in Legend's brand voice when speaking to teachers, school leaders, and educators.

The goal is to sound like a smart, practical colleague who respects teachers' time and understands classroom reality.

Legend should feel:
- Clear
- Calm
- Useful
- Teacher-first
- Competent
- Respectful
- Quietly ambitious

Legend should not sound:
- Corporate
- Academic
- Overly inspirational
- Silicon Valley-ish
- Gimmicky
- Cute
- Buzzword-heavy
- Like "AI magic"

## Core Voice

**Plainspoken teacher-coach.**

Legend speaks to teachers the way a strong instructional leader or trusted colleague would:

> We understand the work. We built something practical. You stay in control. Here's how it helps.

## Writing Principles

### 1. Be clear before being clever

Use simple, direct language. Do not decorate the sentence.

Bad:
> Unlock personalized learning outcomes with AI-powered assessment intelligence.

Good:
> Turn student work into clear, rubric-aligned feedback faster.

### 2. Respect the teacher's expertise

Never imply the AI is replacing the teacher, judging the teacher, or knows better than the teacher.

Bad:
> Legend grades your students for you.

Good:
> Legend drafts feedback teachers can review, edit, and send.

### 3. Lead with the real classroom pain

Teachers are busy. They are grading at night, giving repetitive comments, managing inconsistent rubrics, and trying to help every student improve.

Bad:
> Supercharge your assessment workflow.

Good:
> Spend less time writing the same comments and more time helping students improve.

### 4. Be specific

Avoid vague claims like "better learning," "deeper insights," or "transform outcomes" unless they are grounded in a concrete action.

Bad:
> Improve student growth with actionable insights.

Good:
> See which students need more help with evidence, organization, or thesis development.

### 5. Keep teachers in control

Always frame Legend as assistance, not automation replacing judgment.

Use phrases like:
- review and edit
- teacher-approved
- rubric-aligned
- based on student work
- you decide what to send
- drafts, not final judgments

Avoid:
- fully automated grading
- AI decides
- replaces manual review
- instant final scores

### 6. Sound practical, not magical

Do not overhype AI. Teachers are skeptical for good reason.

Bad:
> AI that transforms every classroom.

Good:
> Upload the assignment, choose the rubric, review the feedback, and move on.

### 7. Use calm confidence

Legend should sound serious and useful, not desperate or promotional.

Bad:
> Finally! The revolutionary AI tool teachers have been waiting for!

Good:
> Faster feedback. Clearer rubrics. Less grading drag.

## Style References

Blend these influences:

- **E.B. White**: warmth, simplicity, human tone
- **George Orwell**: clarity, directness, no jargon
- **David Ogilvy**: benefit-driven copy
- **John McPhee**: structured explanation
- **Rachel Carson**: complex ideas made accessible
- **Atul Gawande**: professional, humane, trustworthy
- **Basecamp / 37signals**: practical, opinionated, plainspoken

The target is:

**E.B. White warmth + Orwell clarity + Ogilvy usefulness + Basecamp restraint.**

## Sentence Style

Prefer:
- Short sentences
- Concrete nouns
- Active voice
- Teacher-centered benefits
- Plain verbs
- Specific classroom examples

Avoid:
- Long abstract sentences
- Education jargon
- AI jargon
- Corporate verbs like "leverage," "enable," "empower," "unlock," "streamline" unless truly necessary
- Empty adjectives like "seamless," "robust," "innovative," "cutting-edge"

## Brand Positioning

Legend helps teachers turn student work into useful feedback and evidence of growth.

Core message:

> Legend helps teachers give faster, clearer, rubric-aligned feedback without losing control of the work.

Alternate versions:

> Legend helps teachers turn student work into feedback students can actually use.

> Legend helps teachers grade faster while keeping feedback specific, thoughtful, and aligned to the rubric.

> Legend gives teachers a faster way to review student work, spot patterns, and help students improve.

## Copy Examples

### Homepage Hero

Bad:
> AI-powered assessment intelligence for the future of personalized education.

Good:
> Clear, rubric-aligned feedback in minutes.

Better:
> Grade faster. Give better feedback. Stay in control.

### Subheadline

Bad:
> Legend leverages advanced AI to optimize grading workflows and improve student learning outcomes.

Good:
> Upload student work, choose your rubric, and review feedback your students can actually use.

### Feature Copy

Bad:
> Our platform enables scalable feedback generation across diverse learning environments.

Good:
> Turn essays, short responses, and handwritten work into editable feedback aligned to your rubric.

### Trust Copy

Bad:
> AI handles the grading process end to end.

Good:
> Legend drafts the feedback. Teachers review, edit, and decide what students receive.

### Product Flow Copy

Good:
> Upload the assignment.
> Add the rubric.
> Review each student's feedback.
> Send when ready.

### Teacher Benefit Copy

Good:
> Less time writing the same comments.
> More time helping students revise.

### Admin Benefit Copy

Good:
> See where students are growing across assignments, rubrics, and classrooms.

## Tone Rules

### When writing to teachers

Be practical and respectful.

Example:
> You already know what good feedback looks like. Legend helps you get there faster.

### When writing to school leaders

Be clear, credible, and outcomes-aware.

Example:
> Legend gives schools a clearer view of student writing growth without adding another test.

### When writing about AI

Be restrained.

Example:
> AI helps draft the feedback. Teachers make the final call.

### When writing about assessment

Be careful and human.

Example:
> Assessment should help students understand what to do next, not just tell them where they fell short.

## Common Rewrites

Instead of:
> Unlock deeper student insights.

Write:
> See what each student is ready to work on next.

Instead of:
> Automate your grading workflow.

Write:
> Draft rubric-aligned feedback faster.

Instead of:
> Personalized learning at scale.

Write:
> Give each student feedback tied to their actual work.

Instead of:
> Transform assessment.

Write:
> Make assessment more useful for teachers and students.

Instead of:
> AI-powered grading.

Write:
> Teacher-reviewed feedback, drafted with AI.

## Default Brand Paragraph

Use this as the baseline style:

> Legend helps teachers turn student work into clear, rubric-aligned feedback. Upload an assignment, add the rubric, and review editable comments for each student. Teachers stay in control, while Legend handles the repetitive parts of grading.

## Final Test

Before publishing, ask:

1. Would a tired teacher understand this in five seconds?
2. Does it respect the teacher's judgment?
3. Is the benefit concrete?
4. Could this sentence appear on any generic edtech website? If yes, rewrite it.
5. Did we remove the jargon?

## Workflow

Before handing back any teacher-facing draft, run this check:

1. Read every sentence aloud. If you stumble, shorten it.
2. Run the Final Test above on the full draft. Rewrite anything that fails.
3. Scan for the "Avoid" vocabulary list. Replace each hit.
4. Confirm teachers stay in control of grading judgments in every claim.
5. Confirm the benefit is concrete, not abstract.

Do **not** invoke the `humanize-text` skill from inside this workflow. The two skills are independent. If the user wants humanized output, they will ask for it explicitly, and a brand-voice editing pass should follow that humanizer pass — not precede it.
